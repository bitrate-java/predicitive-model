The Problem Space

Challenges in PV Solar Generation

Operating large-scale solar arrays requires high-efficiency performance over a 25-year lifespan. Gradual panel degradation, localized hot spots, and soil cover severely limit productivity.

01

Unexpected Panel Outages

Sudden electrical faults, bypass diode short-circuits, or extreme hotspot failures lead to immediate drops in power output, making physical inspections slow and costly.

02

Generation Outage Downtime

Unplanned outages in sub-arrays or tracker lockups block energy export to the grid, resulting in massive financial losses and reduced generation efficiency.

03

Emergency Repair Costs

Dispatching maintenance crews for unscheduled truck rolls without precise diagnostic data incurs high labor costs and prolonged generation downtime.

04

Accelerated Panel Degradation

Failing to address localized cell defects or hotspots leads to accelerated thermal degradation, shortening a panel's standard 25-year useful life.

05

Manual Field Inspection Limits

Periodic physical site surveys using hand-held thermal cameras are labor-intensive, hazardous, and fail to spot sub-surface micro-cracks in solar cells in a timely manner.

Strategic Vision

Project Evolution Roadmap

From basic software simulation algorithms to full scale hardware and production-ready IIoT deployments.

PHASE 1

MATLAB Simulation

Designing physical plant model in Simulink. Injecting structural degradations to export high-fidelity validation data datasets.

PHASE 2

Hardware Prototype

Assembling a micro-scale conveyor model with integrated DC motors, bearings, and temperature modules to mimic a real assembly track.

PHASE 3

ESP32 Sensor Integration

Deploying high-speed ESP32 boards equipped with shunt current sensors and DS18B20 temperature probes to pipe analog telemetry.

PHASE 4

Cloud-Based Monitoring

Configuring MQTT brokers to route sensor payloads securely into cloud-based databases for continuous remote accessibility.

PHASE 5

Mobile Application

Developing lightweight responsive diagnostic applications for factory engineers to receive real-time push alerts on equipment states.

PHASE 6

Digital Twin Technology

Synthesizing 3D WebGL plant representations displaying synchronized spatial sensor mapping in response to real-time physical telemetry.

PHASE 7

Industrial Deployment

Adapting interface protocols (Modbus, OPC UA) to pipe telemetry directly from production lines into our ML diagnostic pipelines.
